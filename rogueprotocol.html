Rogue Protocol

Martha Wells

Set in a space-age universe where corporations hold a lot of power and influence, Rogue Protocol continues the escapades of a snarky rogue SecUnit (a cybernetic AI programmed to perform security measures). Finding out that the too big to fail corporation GrayCris is under investigation (main villains in the first book) the SecUnit sets off to gather evidence that can be used against GrayCris. It does not take long, however, before he runs into another group of people that need his help.

This is the third installment in the Murderbot Diaries series; I am a fan of this series. Compared to the first two short novels, I wasn’t particularly wild about this story, it felt like I was reading filler. The main plot was a bit convoluted, although the author, Martha Wells, has a tendency to get lost in the details while world-building. I also could not bring myself to care about the side characters, but the SecUnit's interactions with them are spot on as usual. I am hoping that Wells' universe gets made into a TV series someday (I would definitely binge-watch it). Although the third installment wasn’t as strong as its predecessors, I still think that readers should give the Murderbot Diaries a shot, particularly if they're fans of snarky, relatable main characters.
